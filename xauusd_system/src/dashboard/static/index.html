How this algorithm works
It buys gold when a strong uptrend breaks to a new high — and gets out the moment that trend stops paying.
No predictions, no AI guessing. Just five plain rules checked every hour. The bot only acts when the market is trending hard and price punches above its recent ceiling. Everything else is about not losing money while it waits.
01
🌊
Read the weather
Is gold in an uptrend and moving with conviction? If the market is choppy or flat, the bot stays out entirely.
→
02
🎯
Wait for the break
It watches the highest price of the last 5 hours (20 × 15-min bars). The instant price closes above that ceiling, that's the buy signal.
→
03
🛡️
Size & protect
It risks only 1% of the account per trade and sets a hard stop-loss right away. Losses are capped before they happen.
→
04
🚪
Ride, then exit
It trails the stop behind the trend. When price falls back through the recent floor, it's out — keeping the gains.
The five rules, in plain english
Filter

Only trade in a trend

Gold has to be above its 200-bar (50-hour) moving average and the trend strength gauge (ADX) has to read above 25. If not, the bot does nothing.

Close > SMA(200) && ADX(14) ≥ 25
Filter

Skip the chaos

If the market is unusually violent — more than twice its normal hourly range — the bot refuses to enter. Wild volatility means unreliable breakouts.

vol_ratio = ATR / 100-bar median ATR < 2.0×
Entry

Buy the breakout

When price closes above the highest point of the last 5 hours, the trend just made a new high — the bot buys and joins the move.

close > Donchian(20) high → ENTER LONG
Risk

Never bet the farm

Each trade risks just 1% of the account. The position size is calculated automatically from the stop distance, so the dollar risk is always the same.

qty = equity × 0.01 / (ATR × 2)
Exit

Cut losers fast

A hard stop sits two ATRs below entry from the very first second. If the breakout fails, the bot is out with a small, known loss.

hard stop = entry − 2 × ATR(14)
Exit

Let winners run

As the trade profits, a trailing stop follows price up. When price closes back below the 2.5-hour low (Donchian 10-bar), the bot banks the gain and exits.

exit when close < Donchian(10) low
