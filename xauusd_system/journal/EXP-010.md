**TRENDING_BULL PF 1.15 with 867 trades** — strongest bull-regime result in the series.
Progression: 0.94 (EXP-006) → 1.06 (EXP-007) → **1.15 (EXP-010)**.

In long-only mode, TRENDING_BEAR drops from 729 trades to only 34. These 34 are LONG
entries that fired during a bear-trending regime — essentially counter-trend longs —
and predictably they lose (PF 0.08). The regime filter mostly prevents this, but a
small number slip through at regime transition boundaries.

CHOPPY and HIGH_VOL together: 35 trades, both losing. These are the regime filter
edges — the ADX threshold prevents most but not all entries in these conditions.

**The system is cleanly TRENDING_BULL at 92.6% of all trades (867/936).**

### CUT 5 — Entry timing (false breakout rate)

```
Total entries analysed: 936
Bar+1 reverted through entry: 702 (75.0%)
Bar+1 AND bar+2 reverted:     603 (64.4%)

Group                 Count   Win%   Avg R
──────────────────────────────────────────
Reverted bar+1          702  25.4%   -0.11
Held bar+1              234  58.1%   +0.50
```

**False-breakout rate: 75.0%** — lowest in the research series.

| Experiment | False-breakout rate | Reverted Avg R | Held Avg R |
|------------|---------------------|---------------|-----------|
| EXP-006 (d=20, c=1, both) | 79.2% | -0.25 | +0.54 |
| EXP-008 (d=40, c=1, both) | 79.3% | -0.23 | +0.56 |
| EXP-007 (d=20, c=3, both) | 77.0% | -0.18 | +0.49 |
| **EXP-010 (d=20, c=3, long-only)** | **75.0%** | **-0.11** | **+0.50** |

Long-only entries achieve a fundamentally lower false-breakout rate. This makes
structural sense: LONG Donchian-20 breakouts in a TRENDING_BULL regime are
trend-aligned. The market is already in an uptrend; an upside breakout has momentum
support. LONG entries in all regimes (EXP-007) include counter-trend longs in bear
phases, which naturally have higher reversion rates.

Equally important: when false breakouts DO occur on long-only entries, they're much
shallower (-0.11 Avg R vs -0.25 in EXP-006). In trending conditions, a failed upside
breakout tends to mean the market goes sideways rather than crashing — the stop-out
is near breakeven rather than at full -1R.

The "held" group (234 trades, 58.1% win, +0.50 Avg R) has the highest win rate in
the series. These are high-quality entries.

## The M15 Long-Only Structural Model

After EXP-010, the complete M15 structural model is:

**The viable variant:**
`long_only=True, entry_confirmation_bars=3, donchian_entry=20, adx_threshold=25`

**Why it works:**
1. TRENDING_BULL regime filter selects for trending market conditions
2. Long-only removes the structurally weak short side (TRENDING_BEAR PF 0.65-0.75)
3. Confirmation=3 reduces entry damage when false breakouts occur
4. Long entries in uptrends have lower base false-breakout rate (75% vs 79%)
5. Failed long entries in uptrends are shallower (market drifts rather than crashes)

**What remains hard:**
- Strip-top-3 PF 0.97 — profitability depends partially on catching large gold moves
- Max DD -35.5% — needs M15-calibrated risk parameters for live deployment
- The 4–8 bar dead zone (191 trades, 0 wins) is structural and irreducible
- 38.8% of trades exit in ≤8 bars and lose — unavoidable false-breakout cost

## What this rules out / confirms

**Confirms:**
- M15 XAU/USD has structural edge on the long side in trending conditions
- Confirmation=3 + long_only is a viable first-generation M15 strategy
- The base system (strip-top-3) is nearly self-sustaining (PF 0.97)
- Both exit types are profitable — the system structure is internally consistent

**Rules out:**
- The short side (TRENDING_BEAR) has structural edge at these parameter settings
- A combined (both-direction) system can overcome the bear-side drag without
  additional filters
