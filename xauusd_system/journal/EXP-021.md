# EXP-021 — H1 Trend Gate + Session Filter (Stacked, 2020-2025)

**Date:** 2026-06-07  
**Status:** COMPLETE — NEGATIVE (regression vs EXP-020)  
**Config:** EXP-020 (H1 trend gate) + `session_filter=true`, research_mode=True  
**Data:** 2020-01-01 → 2025-12-31, 207,936 bars  

---

## Hypothesis

EXP-019 (session filter alone) improved 2021 and 2022 by blocking Asian-session noise entries,
but destroyed 2020 and 2025 by blocking entries during macro events outside London/NY hours.
EXP-020 (H1 trend gate) may have already removed many of the bad entries from 2020 and 2025,
so the session filter's damage might be less severe on this cleaner entry set.

**Sequencing context:** This experiment was run AFTER EXP-022 (cycle durability test). EXP-022
showed the H1 gate does NOT provide sufficient bear market protection (2013-2019 total: -$22,935,
outside the -$10k to +$5k target). However, EXP-021 was run to quantify the combined effect.
Per the session framework: if EXP-022 had failed the cycle test, EXP-021 would be "optimising
a system with a structural cycle problem." It did fail.

## Analysis

### 1. 2025 destroyed (-$30,369)

The April 2025 gold ATH run — the single most valuable event for this system — was heavily
filtered by the session restriction. The April 2025 entry that was worth +$14,124 in EXP-020
does NOT appear in EXP-021's top 3 (which are 2023 and 2024 trades). The session filter blocked
the ATH entry because the decisive breakout bar closed outside London/NY hours.

2025 went from +$48,704 (EXP-020) to +$18,335 (EXP-021). A $30,369 reduction in what was the
best year in the backtest. This is not a minor trimming — it's structural destruction of the
system's primary profit mechanism.

### 2. 2021 and 2024 improved — the filter's intended effect

The session filter does what it was designed to do:
- 2021: -$16,217 → -$6,449 (+$9,768) — blocked Asian-session false breakouts in the gold decline
- 2024: -$20,694 → -$7,329 (+$13,365) — blocked grinding 2024 entries outside active sessions

These improvements are real. The problem is that the filter cannot distinguish "Asian-session
noise in a grinding year" from "Asian-session macro event in an explosive year."

### 3. Trade count borderline (395)

The combined H1 gate + session filter retained only 395/936 baseline trades (42%). This is above
the ≥350 threshold but the PF estimate at 395 trades carries meaningful sampling variance.
The strip-top-3 PF of 0.924 would need to be ~0.965 to give confidence of exceeding 1.0 in
an equivalent out-of-sample period.

### 4. 2023 significantly hurt (-$10,217)

EXP-019's session filter had reduced 2023 in isolation. In combination with the H1 gate,
2023 drops from +$22,988 to +$12,771. The Middle East gold spike (Oct 2023) fire was captured
(+$14,391 — it's the top winner), but other 2023 entries were blocked.

### 5. Why strip-top-3 PF is 0.924 vs EXP-020's 0.98

The session filter removed many winning trades (mainly 2023 and 2025) without proportionally
removing losing trades. The base system (with neither filter) has structural losers (false
breakouts) that occur throughout all sessions. By restricting to London/NY, you concentrate
the sample but the false-breakout rate doesn't fall enough to compensate for the missed macro
event entries.

---

## Verdict: NEGATIVE — SESSION FILTER ADDS COMPLEXITY WITHOUT NET BENEFIT

**Context from EXP-022:** The H1 gate failed the cycle durability test (2013-2019 total:
-$22,935, outside the -$10k to +$5k target). Therefore, EXP-021 represents stacking complexity
on a system that already has a structural bear market problem.

Even ignoring the cycle issue: the combined EXP-021 result (strip-top-3 PF 0.924) is strictly
worse than EXP-020 alone (0.98). The session filter regression (-0.056 PF, -$24k net P&L) is
too large to accept.

**EXP-020 (H1 trend gate alone) remains the best single-filter configuration.**
