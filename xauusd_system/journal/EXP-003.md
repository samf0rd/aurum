## Key findings

1. **Trade count barely changed (144 → 139)** — confirmation=2 adds almost no
   additional filtering beyond confirmation=1. The extra bar requirement is not
   a meaningful selector.

2. **Bar+1 reversion increased to 86.3%** — further confirmation degrades entry
   timing on winning trades. The `held bar+1` cohort shrank from 32 to 19 entries
   while their win rate stayed constant (50% → 52.6%), meaning fewer good entries
   are being captured.

3. **Signal exit PF collapsed (0.88 → 0.65)** — the extra delay is giving back
   part of the move before entry even happens.

4. **TRENDING_BEAR PF: 0.53** (EXP-002: 0.61, baseline: 1.16). Short side is
   consistently underwater on H1 across all three experiments. Confirmed timeframe
   effect — M15 showed short PF 1.21.

5. **TRENDING_BULL PF: 1.27** (EXP-002: 1.49). Bull-side is the edge carrier but
   confirmation=2 costs 0.22 PF points vs confirmation=1 on this side too.

## Verdict
INCONCLUSIVE — this experiment's purpose (find diminishing returns) succeeded.
Confirmation=1 is the sweet spot. Confirmation=2 degrades both PF and strip-top-3
PF while providing almost no additional trade filtering.

The most important finding is structural: TRENDING_BEAR has been 0.53–0.61 across
all three H1 experiments. Short-side drag costs ~0.2–0.3 PF points on the combined
number. Next step is to isolate the bull-side edge cleanly.
