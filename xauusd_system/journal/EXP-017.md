# EXP-017 — ATR Volatility Floor Filter (2020–2025)

**Date:** 2026-06-07  
**Status:** COMPLETE — NEGATIVE (filter hurts net P&L)  
**Config:** EXP-010 + `atr_vol_filter=True`, `atr_vol_lookback=12096` (6-month M15 window)  
**Data:** 2020-01-01 → 2025-12-31, 207,936 bars  

---

## Hypothesis

EXP-013 revealed 2024 was -$17,318 despite gold +27% that year. 2025 was +$43,915. The difference appeared to be **volatility regime**: 2024 was a gradual grind higher, 2025 was explosive (ATH breakouts). Hypothesis: a floor filter — only enter when current ATR/Close is above its 6-month rolling median — would gate out grinding 2024 periods while preserving 2025 explosive entries.

---

## Implementation Note (Bug Found and Fixed)

**First run was bugged.** The backtest engine passes `MAX_WINDOW=500` bars to `on_bar()`. The filter's warmup check is `if len(bars) < lookback + 16` (i.e., `< 12112`). Since 500 < 12112, this always returned `True` (don't block). First run: 936 trades — identical to EXP-010.

**Fix:** `DonchianBreakoutSignalGenerator` now maintains a `deque(maxlen=12116)` fed by a new `accumulate(bar)` method called by the engine on every bar (regardless of position state). The filter uses this internal full history instead of the truncated 500-bar window.

## Analysis

### 1. 2024 improved (+$5,290) but every positive year got worse

2024 went from -$17,318 to -$12,028, a $5,290 improvement — consistent with the hypothesis. However, the filter also degraded every profitable year: 2020 -$9,090, 2023 -$9,230, 2025 -$9,126. Since the system is right-skewed (profits driven by large wins), cutting winners hurts more than reducing losers helps. Net: **-$20,318 worse than baseline**.

### 2. The filter is not selective for the right distinction

The hypothesis assumed "2024 = low ATR, 2025 = high ATR." Reality: the 6-month rolling ATR median captures volatility at the **bar level**, but the distinction between a "grinding bull year" and an "explosive bull year" is a **macro regime** phenomenon. Both 2024 and 2025 contained individual bars with both high and low ATR. The filter applies similarly to both years, removing ~21% of trades uniformly rather than cleanly separating the two regimes.

### 3. Trade count: 740 vs 936 (–21%)

The filter blocks 196/936 = 21% of entry signals. This is less than the expected 50% because the system already pre-selects for volatile conditions (SMA regime + ADX≥25 + 3-bar confirmation). The remaining 936 signals from EXP-010 are already concentrated in higher-volatility periods — the additional ATR floor only acts marginally.

### 4. Strip-top-3 improved slightly

| Config   | Strip-top-3 PF | Strip-top-3 Net |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|
| EXP-010  | ~0.94         | ~-$16k          |
| EXP-017  | 0.94          | -$18,982        |

Strip-top-3 PF is essentially unchanged. The base distribution quality didn't improve.

### 5. TRENDING_BULL PF improved (1.09 → 1.12)

The filter does improve quality within bull regime entries (682 trades, PF=1.12 vs ~1.09 in EXP-010). But this gain is masked by the proportional removal of winners.

### 6. False breakout rate improved slightly

- EXP-010: 79.0% false breakout rate
- EXP-017: 76.4% false breakout rate (–2.6pp)

A small but real improvement — the filter does gate out some of the lowest-quality breakouts.

---

## Verdict: DO NOT USE this filter at current configuration

The ATR floor filter at 6-month lookback is **not an improvement** over the baseline. It reduces losses modestly in grinding years but reduces gains more in explosive years. The hypothesis was directionally correct (2024 improved) but the magnitude was wrong — the filter is too coarse to separate the regimes the user actually cares about.

### What would be needed to make a macro regime filter work

The distinction the user wants to capture ("grinding 2024" vs "explosive 2025") is not visible at the individual-bar level. It requires:

1. **A quarterly/annual ATR regime metric**, not a bar-by-bar gate. E.g.: only deploy the strategy when the trailing 3-month realized ATR is above its 3-year median. This gates entire calendar periods, not individual trades.

2. **A gold-specific volatility index** (GVZ, the gold VIX equivalent). When GVZ > some threshold, deploy; when below, stay flat. This is forward-looking implied vol, not backward-looking realized vol.

3. **A bear-market regime filter** (per EXP-016 findings): since the system fails completely in 2013-2019, a D1 SMA/trend filter that detects secular gold bear markets would eliminate the truly catastrophic periods.

### Connection to EXP-016

EXP-016 showed the system loses -$40,543 over 2013-2019 (gold secular bear). The ATR floor filter applied to 2013-2019 data might be more powerful there — in a grinding bear market, ATR/Close ratios may persistently be below their median, gating out most entries. Testing this is the natural next step (EXP-018 candidate).
