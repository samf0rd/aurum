## 5-Cut Diagnostic

### CUT 1 — P&L by regime

```
Regime             Trades   Win%     PF  Avg R Win Avg R Loss
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CHOPPY                 40  22.5%   0.21      +0.46      -0.63
HIGH_VOL               59  40.7%   0.52      +0.98      -0.88
TRENDING_BEAR         846  25.8%   0.65      +1.40      -0.72
TRENDING_BULL        1070  31.6%   0.94      +1.51      -0.71
```

**Key finding:** TRENDING_BULL is nearly breakeven at PF 0.94 with 1070 trades.
TRENDING_BEAR is PF 0.65 with 846 trades. CHOPPY and HIGH_VOL are noise-level trade
counts (40 and 59) — the regime filter is correctly suppressing most non-trending bars.

The R/R shape on both trend regimes is acceptable: winners ~+1.4-1.5R, losers ~-0.7R
(approximately 2:1). The problem is win rate — 26-32% when breakeven on 2:1 R/R
requires 33%+. TRENDING_BULL at 31.6% is one percentage point shy of breakeven.

Compare to H1 EXP-002: TRENDING_BULL PF 1.49 (38%+ win rate). The M15 version has
roughly the same R/R shape but 6-7 fewer winning percentage points. This is the
false-breakout penalty — M15 bars are much shorter and more noise-prone.

### CUT 2 — P&L by exit type

```
Exit type           Count   Win%     PF   Avg R
───────────────────────────────────────────────
signal_exit           299  32.8%   0.64   -0.10
stop_out             1716  28.6%   0.80   -0.09
```

Counterintuitive: stop-outs have *higher* PF (0.80) than signal exits (0.64). Both
have nearly identical Avg R (~-0.10). This means the trailing exit (Donchian-10) is
not effectively capturing winners — when a trade survives long enough to hit the
trailing stop, it's often being exited at a marginal or negative point.

On H1, the trailing exit works because trends run for many hours. On M15, a Donchian-10
trailing exit fires after 10 * 15min = 2.5 hours, which may be cutting winners short
in fast-moving markets. The signal exits have slightly higher win rate (32.8% vs 28.6%)
but worse PF, suggesting they occasionally catch big moves but more often exit small
winners before they fully develop.

**Implication:** The exit mechanism may need M15-native calibration (longer trailing
window, e.g. Donchian-20 exit, or time-based hold).

### CUT 3 — Bars-in-trade distribution

```
Duration      Count  Wins   Avg R
─────────────────────────────────
1 bar           221    42   -0.62
2–3 bars        252     7   -0.88
4–8 bars        347     0   -0.87
9–24 bars       805   270   -0.03
25–72 bars      293   261   +1.78
>72 bars          9     9   +8.19
```

**This is the most important cut.** Three distinct zones:

**Zone 1 — Pure noise (1–8 bars, 820 trades): all losing.**
- 4–8 bar bucket: **zero wins in 347 trades.** Every single 4–8 bar trade stops out.
- These trades enter, immediately prove themselves false breakouts, and stop out at
  full -1R within a few bars. This is the false-breakout penalty manifesting structurally.

**Zone 2 — Dead weight (9–24 bars, 805 trades): flat at -0.03 Avg R.**
- 40% of all trades, essentially zero edge. These trades survive 2–6 hours but trend
  pressure weakens and they stop out near breakeven.

**Zone 3 — Structural edge (25+ bars, 302 trades): highly profitable.**
- 25–72 bars: 261/293 wins (89%), Avg R +1.78. These are the real trades.
- >72 bars: 9/9 wins (100%), Avg R +8.19. Massive multi-day runners.
- **302 trades with near-100% win rate would produce a very high PF in isolation.**

The structural picture: M15 Donchian breakouts either work immediately and sustain
(25+ bars, ~15% of all entries) or fail within 2–24 bars (85% of entries). The system
is profitable only on the 15% that sustains — the other 85% are structural losers.

If you could somehow select only the 25+ bar trades ex ante, this becomes an
excellent system. The core research question is: **what predicts whether a breakout
will sustain?**


The top-3 outliers are distributed across different years (2020, 2021, 2023) and both
directions (2 shorts, 1 long). This is significantly better than EXP-005's COVID-only
outliers — the system CAN catch genuine structural moves across different market regimes.

The Oct 2023 LONG (R=+24.82) corresponds to the gold breakout above $1950 into the
Israel-Hamas war open (gold's biggest 3-day move of 2023). The Aug 2020 SHORT is
the gold correction from all-time high above $2,000. The Sep 2021 SHORT is the
correction from the $1,920 bull trap.

PF drops from 0.78 to 0.72 without these 3 trades — the system still loses, but
the tail risk isn't entirely dependent on one lucky period. The distribution of
large winners across years is mildly encouraging.

### CUT 5 — Entry timing (false breakout rate)

```
Total entries analysed: 2015
Bar+1 reverted through entry: 1595 (79.2%)
Bar+1 AND bar+2 reverted:     1386 (68.8%)

Group                 Count   Win%   Avg R
──────────────────────────────────────────
Reverted bar+1         1595  22.6%   -0.25
Held bar+1              420  54.3%   +0.54
```

**The defining finding of EXP-006.** The 420 trades (20.8%) where the market held
above the entry bar (did not immediately reverse) produced:
- Win rate: **54.3%** (vs 33% needed for breakeven on 2:1 R/R)
- Avg R: **+0.54** — each entry profitable on expectation

The 1,595 reverted trades produced:
- Win rate: 22.6%
- Avg R: -0.25 — each entry loses on expectation

The false breakout rate (79.2%) is stable across all experiments: EXP-001 (M15, ~80%),
EXP-002 H1 (~78%), EXP-005 (80.8%), EXP-006 (79.2%). This is a structural property of
Donchian-20 breakouts in XAU/USD, not something that changes with timeframe.

The system can't know at entry which group a trade belongs to. But higher confirmation
requirements can increase the proportion of "held" trades — at the cost of missing
some early moves. **This is the core hypothesis for EXP-007.**

## M15 Structural Identity

After EXP-006, the M15 structure is clear:

**Donchian-20 breakouts on M15 are 79% false breakouts.** The 21% that hold are
highly profitable (54.3% win, +0.54R expectation). The 79% that reverse are
consistently negative (-0.25R expectation). The system's current PF (0.78) is the
weighted average of these two populations.

**The path to edge**: find a pre-entry filter that increases the proportion of
"held" breakouts without requiring lookahead. Candidates:

1. **More confirmation bars** — confirmation=2 or 3 forces the market to prove it
   held for multiple bars before triggering entry. This is the cleanest test.
   Risk: delay causes worse entry price on real moves.

2. **Stricter trend filter** — ADX≥30 or ADX≥35 reduces total entry count but should
   bias toward stronger trends where breakouts sustain longer.

3. **Wider Donchian** — donchian_entry=40 means a 40-bar (10-hour) channel breakout.
   These are stronger structural breaks, more likely to sustain.

4. **Direction isolation** — CUT 1 shows TRENDING_BULL PF 0.94 (near breakeven) vs
   TRENDING_BEAR PF 0.65. Is the bull-trend LONG side actually profitable if run alone?
   Is the bear-trend SHORT side dragging the system down?

**The 4–8 bar zero-win zone** is a hard constraint: any trade that enters and doesn't
move immediately dies. This means the winning trades need to be the kind that gap or
accelerate from entry. Wider Donchian (longer-term breakout) should produce more of
these momentum moves.

## What this rules out

- M15 is not a broken timeframe. PF 0.78 over 2015 trades with a clear profitable
  subgroup (held entries) means there IS structural edge — it just needs filtering.
- The system is not lottery-dependent. Top-3 covers years 2020, 2021, 2023 — not
  a single crisis window.
- H1 risk parameters are completely incompatible with M15 (confirmed by EXP-005).

## What EXP-006 does tell us

1. **The edge is real but narrow**: 20.8% of entries (420/2015) are genuinely good.
2. **The 4–8 bar dead zone is absolute**: 0/347 wins. No entry should be in this range.
3. **The short side appears weaker** (TRENDING_BEAR PF 0.65 vs TRENDING_BULL PF 0.94).
4. **25+ bar trades have 89%+ win rate** — these are where the money is.
5. **The false breakout rate (79%) is fundamental to M15 XAU/USD structure.**
