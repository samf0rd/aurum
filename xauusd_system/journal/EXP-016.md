**Date:** 2026-06-07  
**Status:** COMPLETE — NEGATIVE  
**Config:** EXP-010 strategy + research_mode=True (same as EXP-010 baseline)  
**Data:** Dukascopy M15 XAUUSD, 2013-01-01 → 2019-12-31, 165,043 bars  

---

## Hypothesis

Gold 2013–2019 was a secular bear / consolidation cycle. Long-only Donchian breakouts should underperform relative to 2020–2025 (the bull cycle). Key question was **magnitude**: does the system lose -$5k/year (tolerable) or -$30k/year (disqualifying)?

Year-by-year prediction going in:
- 2013: negative (gold crashed -30%)
- 2014: negative (choppy downward)
- 2015: negative (gold → $1,050 lows)
- 2016: possibly positive (Brexit spike)
- 2017–2018: negative (slow market)
- 2019: positive (June-Sep +$250 breakout)

## Key Findings

### 1. The magnitude is disqualifying

2013 alone destroys -$33,623 — nearly a third of starting capital in year one. Gold's 2013 crash (-30%) generates relentless false upside breakouts that all reverse. With entry_confirmation_bars=3, some whipsaws are filtered but the trend is down hard and every long entry eventually stops out.

The question was "$5k/year or $30k/year in drawdown?" The answer is -$33,623 in a single year — catastrophically worse than the worst case.

### 2. Prediction accuracy was mixed

- 2013 negative ✓ — correct
- 2014 **positive** — wrong. Two enormous outlier trades (2014-06-12: +$6,530, 2014-06-19: +$13,498 in the same week) saved the year. Strip top-3 and 2014 becomes negative. The edge was two lucky geopolitical spikes, not edge.
- 2015 negative ✓ — correct
- 2016 negative — wrong prediction (expected positive from Brexit). The June 2016 Brexit spike *did* happen, but either the confirmation bars requirement missed the initial entry or the move structure didn't generate a clean Donchian breakout in time.
- 2017 **positive** — wrong prediction. The 2017 gold rally (gradual) produced enough Donchian breakouts to generate edge — unexpected.
- 2018 barely positive — manageable, not as bad as expected
- 2019 negative — wrong (expected positive from June-Sep rally). The +$250 gold breakout is confirmed real, but apparently the system didn't capture it efficiently at M15 with 3-bar confirmation.

### 3. Strip-top-3 is decisive

Removing the three largest wins (2014-06-19 +$13,498, 2014-06-12 +$6,530, 2018-10-11 +$5,539):
- Remaining PF: **0.81** — the base system is a clear loser
- Net P&L: **-$66,109** on 1156 trades

This confirms that the 2014 "positive year" was entirely carried by two trades in the same week, not repeatable edge.

### 4. Regime stats confirm structural problem

| Regime        | Trades |  Win% |  PF  |
|---------------|--------|-------|------|
| TRENDING_BULL |  1,084 | 30.1% | 0.91 |
| TRENDING_BEAR |     51 | 21.6% | 0.09 |
| HIGH_VOL      |     15 | 33.3% | 0.36 |
| CHOPPY        |      9 | 33.3% | 4.71 |

Even in TRENDING_BULL regime, PF=0.91 — below breakeven. This means the SMA-200 / ADX filter correctly identifies "bull" regime periods but those bull periods inside a secular bear are still dominated by grinding false breakouts.

### 5. Duration analysis

| Duration   | Count | Wins | Avg R |

| 1 bar      |    76 |   21 | -0.52 |
| 2–3 bars   |   131 |    3 | -0.84 |

The pattern holds: short-duration exits are losers, long-duration (trend-following) trades are winners. But in 2013–2019 there simply aren't enough long-duration wins to offset the chop losses. The 186 "25–72 bar" winner trades are the system's edge — but there aren't enough of them.

## Interpretation

The strategy has **regime-specific edge**. It performs when:
1. Gold is in a secular bull market (2020–2025: +$1,500 → $2,800+)
2. Volatility is high enough to generate clean, sustained breakouts

It fails when:
1. Gold is in a secular bear (2013–2015: relentless selling)
2. Gold is in a flat consolidation with high whipsaw (2016–2019)

The 2020–2025 edge is NOT a market structure edge (i.e., "Donchian breakouts always work on gold"). It is a **regime-specific edge** that only applies during high-volatility bull periods.

### Implication for deployment

The system is NOT deployable as a standalone "always on" strategy across gold market cycles. It requires a macro regime gate:
- A bear-market filter (e.g., 200-day SMA slope, multi-month trend confirmation) could avoid deploying 2013-type years
- The ATR volatility floor filter (EXP-017) is a direct attempt to solve this: only trade when current ATR is above its 6-month historical median

### Connection to EXP-017

EXP-016 provides the motivation for EXP-017: if the ATR floor filter correctly identifies "high-volatility" periods as the system's productive regime, it could potentially gate out years like 2013 and 2015 where low ATR/grinding conditions dominate. Testing this on pre-2020 data is the logical next step (EXP-018 candidate).
