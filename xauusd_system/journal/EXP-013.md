## Analysis

### Hypothesis: PARTIALLY CORRECT

Pre-experiment hypothesis: 2021/2022 negative, 2020/2023/2024/2025 positive.

Actual result:
- 2021 negative ✓
- 2022 negative ✓
- 2020 positive ✓
- 2023 positive ✓
- **2024 NEGATIVE** ✗ — the key surprise
- 2025 positive ✓

### The 2024 finding is the most important result of this experiment

Gold in 2024 was in its biggest sustained bull market in decades, rallying from ~$2,070
to ~$2,650, eventually reaching ~$2,800 by year-end. By naive "gold bull market = profit"
logic, 2024 should be the best year in the dataset.

Instead: 2024 is the worst year. PF 0.76, -$17,318, max DD -23.2%.

The explanation is structural. The strategy captures **explosive, discontinuous gold moves**
not **gradual trend-following gains**. 2024 was a grind — gold ratcheted higher in a series
of overlapping breakouts, most of which failed to sustain. Each failure cost ~1R. The one
large 2024 winner (Aug 2024 breakout, ~$7,422 in EXP-012b) was outweighed by losses from
~164 other trades, most of which stopped out.

Contrast with 2025: +$43,915 (PF 1.57) on 177 trades with only -8% standalone DD. The
Apr 2025 trade alone (~$14,124) rode gold's spike through $3,000. When the move is big
and fast, the system wins. When it's gradual, the system loses.

### The correct characterization

This is not a "gold bull market strategy." It is a **gold macro volatility / explosive move
capture system**. The winning condition is:

> Gold must move **fast** AND **far** after a Donchian-20 breakout

Gradual appreciation with many false starts → net negative (2024).
Explosive spike on macro event → large positive outlier (Oct 2023, Apr 2025, July 2020).

## Conclusions

1. **The strategy is not a "gold bull market" strategy.** 2024 disproves the thesis.
   Gold up 27% in 2024 = system loses 17% of equity.

2. **The strategy is an explosive move capture system.** It profits when gold makes
   large, fast, sustained moves that hold beyond a Donchian-20 breakout. These occur
   approximately once per year in favorable years (one large trade per year).

3. **Three years are negative (2021, 2022, 2024).** Three years are positive (2020, 2023,
   2025). The positive years have larger magnitude (+$72k combined vs -$38k combined).
   The asymmetry is what produces the positive 6-year result.

4. **Negative years are survivable.** Worst single year: 2024 at -$17,318 (-17% of
   initial equity, -23.2% standalone DD). The system does not blow up in bad years;
   it grinds down slowly.

5. **2025 alone rescues the portfolio.** Without 2025's +$43,915, the 5-year result
   (2020-2024) is: +$14,434 - $9,017 - $12,179 + $13,822 - $17,318 = **-$10,258**.
   The 2025 year turns a 5-year loser into a 6-year winner.

## Implications for EXP-014 (pre-2020 data)

The 2024 result shows that even within the 2020-2025 "bull market" window, there are
sustained negative years. The pre-2020 hypothesis should be refined:

> **2015-2019 hypothesis (revised):** The system will be negative in 2015-2016 (gold in
> secular bear/consolidation), flat in 2017 (mild gold recovery), and potentially
> negative in 2018 (gold declined). 2019 might be marginally positive if the
> mid-2019 gold breakout produces a large enough spike.

The key question EXP-014 answers is not just "is it negative?" but "does it lose
catastrophically (>30% annual) or manageably (<25%)?"
