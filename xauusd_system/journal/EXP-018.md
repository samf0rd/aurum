# EXP-018 — Vol-Ratio Floor Filter (vol_ratio ≥ 1.3)

**Date:** 2026-06-07  
**Status:** COMPLETE — NEGATIVE  
**Config:** EXP-010 + `vol_ratio_floor=1.3`, research_mode=True  
**Data:** 2020-01-01 → 2025-12-31, 207,936 bars  

---

## Hypothesis

A real breakout comes with above-average volatility. `vol_ratio` = current ATR/Close ÷ median ATR/Close over rolling 100 bars. Requiring `vol_ratio ≥ 1.3` (ATR 30% above median) should gate out low-volatility fake breakouts and improve base trade quality.

## Analysis

### 1. The filter is working backwards

The vol_ratio floor requires current ATR to already be elevated before entry. But the system's best trades are caught by entering WHEN volatility begins expanding — not AFTER it has expanded. At the exact moment of a quality breakout, vol_ratio is often still near 1.0 (the breakout is the START of the volatility increase, not the continuation of it).

This is visible in the year-by-year data: 2020 is catastrophically worse (-$14,605 vs +$14,434 baseline). The March 2020 COVID gold surge started with a volatility transition — the system entered during accumulation when vol_ratio was below 1.3, and those early entries captured the full move. The floor blocked them.

### 2. 2025 is almost entirely wiped out

2025 (baseline +$43,915) becomes +$6,600 — a 85% reduction. The 2025 ATH breakout in April 2025 was a structured, building momentum move, not an already-elevated volatility move. The vol_ratio floor blocked the entry before the explosion.

### 3. False-breakout rate barely moved

76.4% vs 79.0% — only 2.6pp improvement. The vol_ratio floor doesn't significantly separate quality from noise breakouts.

### 4. The vol_ratio cap works; the floor doesn't

The existing `vol_ratio_cap=2.0` (blocking HIGH_VOL regime) is a correct filter because extreme volatility IS dangerous (stops get triggered by spike noise). But the symmetric argument (low vol = fake) doesn't hold — it's volatility expansion from low that creates the best entries.

---

## Verdict: DO NOT USE

This is the worst result of the EXP-018/019/020 batch:
- Strip-top-3 PF = 0.77 (vs threshold of 1.0)
- Net P&L **negative** despite baseline being positive
- 2020 and 2025 (the two best years) are both severely degraded

A variation worth exploring: instead of a static floor, use a *rising* ATR signal — only enter when vol_ratio has increased over the past N bars. This would detect volatility expansion rather than requiring already-elevated volatility.
