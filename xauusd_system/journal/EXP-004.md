## Verdict

**REJECTED.** The hypothesis that TRENDING_BEAR signals were pure drag is wrong.

## Key finding: path dependency

The Aug 29, 2025 R=6.56 trade — worth $6,086, roughly half of EXP-002's gross profit — is completely absent from EXP-004. Trade sequence comparison reveals why:

**EXP-002 (Aug 2025):**
Short trades in July–August cycle the engine flat at multiple points. By Aug 28, a
long exits via signal_exit after 18 bars, leaving the engine flat. The engine
immediately sees the Aug 28 confirmation signal, enters, and that long runs 53 bars
to the R=6.56 exit.

**EXP-004 (Aug 2025):**
No shorts in July–August. The engine enters a long on Aug 22 that loses over 28 bars
(exit Aug 25). Engine becomes flat on Aug 25. The Aug 26 and Aug 28 confirmation
windows pass without entry (the engine may be in position or the fresh-breakout guard
differs). The Aug 29 entry never fires.

**Conclusion:** The short-side activity in EXP-002 keeps the engine cycling and
flat at the right moments. The TRENDING_BULL PF of 1.49 is not decomposable from
the rest of the system — those specific bull trades only fire because the short
side created the position-state required to enter them.

The 1.49 TRENDING_BULL PF is not a floor you can extract by suppressing shorts.
It is a path-dependent property of the full two-sided system.

as a pre-check guard before TRENDING_BEAR regime signals are processed.
