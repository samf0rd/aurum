# EXP-008: M15 wider Donchian — donchian_entry=40 (10-hour channel)

**Date:** 2026-06-07
**Status:** COMPLETE — donchian width is a weak lever; false-breakout rate is structural
**Results file:** results/exp-008.json
**Experiment config:** experiments/exp-008.yaml

## Hypothesis

EXP-006 showed the 4–8 bar zero-win zone is absolute and the 25+ bar trades win 89%+.
Theory: a 40-bar (10-hour) channel breakout represents a stronger structural move —
the market has been compressing longer, so genuine breaks should sustain past 25 bars
more reliably and the false-breakout rate should fall below 79%.

### CUT 5 — Entry timing (false breakout rate)

```
Total entries analysed: 1722
Bar+1 reverted through entry: 1365 (79.3%)
Bar+1 AND bar+2 reverted:     1172 (68.1%)

Group                 Count   Win%   Avg R
──────────────────────────────────────────
Reverted bar+1         1365  22.9%   -0.23
Held bar+1              357  52.7%   +0.56
```

**The definitive finding: false-breakout rate 79.3% — essentially identical to
EXP-006 (79.2%).** A 40-bar channel breakout is just as likely to immediately reverse
as a 20-bar channel breakout on M15 XAU/USD.

This confirms the false-breakout rate (~79%) is a **fundamental structural property**
of M15 XAU/USD Donchian breakouts, not an artifact of channel width. Whether the
price has been ranging for 5 hours or 10 hours, the next bar's reversal probability
is essentially identical.

Comparison across experiments:

| Experiment | Configuration | False-breakout rate |
|------------|---------------|---------------------|
| EXP-001 | H1, long-only | ~80% |
| EXP-002 | H1, conf=1 | 78% |
| EXP-005 | M15, conf=1 (limited data) | 80.8% |
| EXP-006 | M15, conf=1, d=20 | 79.2% |
| EXP-007 | M15, conf=3, d=20 | 77.0% |
| EXP-008 | M15, conf=1, d=40 | **79.3%** |

The false-breakout rate is ~79% across all timeframes and all Donchian widths. Only
confirmation bars nudge it (77.0% in EXP-007 vs 79.2% in EXP-006 — a 2.2pp reduction).

## Verdict: donchian width is a secondary lever

Donchian=40 vs donchian=20 comparison:

| What changed | EXP-006 | EXP-008 | Delta |
|-------------|---------|---------|-------|
| PF | 0.78 | 0.83 | **+0.05** |
| TRENDING_BULL PF | 0.94 | 0.97 | +0.03 |
| False-breakout rate | 79.2% | 79.3% | 0.0pp |
| Strip-top-3 PF | 0.72 | 0.76 | +0.04 |

Confirmation=3 vs confirmation=1 comparison:

| What changed | EXP-006 | EXP-007 | Delta |
|-------------|---------|---------|-------|
| PF | 0.78 | 0.90 | **+0.12** |
| TRENDING_BULL PF | 0.94 | 1.06 | +0.12 |
| False-breakout rate | 79.2% | 77.0% | -2.2pp |
| Strip-top-3 PF | 0.72 | 0.83 | +0.11 |

**Confirmation bars are 2.4× more effective than Donchian width** for improving PF.
The false-breakout rate is structural and cannot be meaningfully reduced by changing
the breakout channel width.

The key structural insight: on M15 XAU/USD, whether a breakout immediately reverses
has nothing to do with how long the price has been in the prior range. It is entirely
a function of market microstructure at the breakout bar itself.

## Implications for EXP-009 and EXP-010

**EXP-009 (confirmation=3 + donchian=40):**
If the effects are additive, expected PF ≈ 0.90 + 0.05 = ~0.95. But since the
false-breakout mechanism is identical (79% structural), the combination is unlikely
to reach PF > 1.0 overall. Worth running to measure the actual combination effect.

**EXP-010 (long-only, confirmation=3, donchian=20):**
Higher priority. EXP-007 showed TRENDING_BULL at PF 1.06 with 909 trades. Removing
TRENDING_BEAR (729 trades, PF 0.75) should push system PF to ~1.06+ with reasonable
trade count. This is the most direct test of a viable M15 strategy variant.
