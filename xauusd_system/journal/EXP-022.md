## Hypothesis

The H1 200-SMA (200 hours ≈ 8 trading days) should function as a natural bear market gate.
During 2013's 30% gold crash, price spent the majority of Apr-Dec 2013 BELOW the H1 SMA-200,
which would block most long entries. The SMA only lags by ~5-6 trading days, so once gold broke
structurally below the SMA, the gate would close quickly.

**Success criterion:** Combined 2013-2019 P&L in the range -$10k to +$5k  
(vs EXP-016 baseline of -$40,543 without the H1 gate)

## Analysis

### 1. Why the H1 gate was insufficient for 2013

The 2013 gold crash (-30% Apr-Dec 2013) improved from -$33,623 to -$24,394 — the gate saved
$9,229 but the year is still catastrophically negative (PF 0.37).

**Root cause — SMA lag at the wrong timescale:** The H1 SMA-200 covers 200 trading hours ≈ 8
trading days. During the 2013 crash, gold didn't fall in a straight line — it declined through
repeated counter-rallies. Each counter-rally would push price back above the H1 SMA-200 for
days at a time, reopening the gate and allowing long entries into what were temporary recoveries
within a secular downtrend.

A 24% trade reduction (1,159 → 879 across 2013-2019) confirms the gate was open during
counter-rallies — those 280 missing trades are the ones filtered by the H1 gate. The 2013
trade reduction from 139 to 87 (37% reduction) shows the gate was active, but 87 trades at
PF 0.37 still produces -$24k.

### 2. 2015 still deeply negative (-$15,869)

2015 was a structural bear year (gold -10%). Despite the H1 gate, 106 trades were taken at
PF 0.63. Same mechanism: counter-rallies temporarily pushed price above H1 SMA-200.

### 3. Surprising improvements in 2017 and 2018

The H1 gate was helpful in both directions:
- 2017 improved from +$8,830 to +$15,084 (+$6,254): cleaner trend confirmation in 2017's
  choppy recovery; fewer counter-trend entries
- 2018 improved from +$1,617 to +$10,072 (+$8,455): 2018 had sharp gold selloffs followed
  by recoveries; the H1 gate correctly waited for confirmed recoveries

### 4. 2019 deteriorated (-$7,635)

2019 saw gold recover from ~$1,175 to ~$1,515. The H1 SMA-200 lagged the initial recovery,
blocking entries during the breakout. The baseline caught early entries; the H1 gate missed
them and entered later into the trend continuation (lower R multiples).

### 5. What would actually gate 2013?

The H1 SMA-200 is 200 HOURS (8 trading days). For a 30% secular gold bear:
- H1 SMA-200 lag period: ~8 trading days
- Counter-rally duration in 2013: frequently 2-4 weeks

A proper macro regime gate needs a MUCH longer lookback:
- **W1 SMA-52** (52 weekly closes = 1 year of weekly closes): 52 weeks lag
- This would stay below the declining SMA for most of 2013-2015 without being reopened
  by multi-week counter-rallies
- Proposed as EXP-023: `w1_trend_gate=True` using 52-week weekly SMA

## Implications for Deployment

This result means the deployed system (EXP-020 config) does NOT have full cycle durability.
During a secular gold bear market of the 2013-2015 type, the system would still incur
significant losses even with the H1 gate active.

**Mitigation applied for paper trading deployment:**  
A manual monitoring rule: **suspend trading if gold spot is >20% below its 24-month high.**
This is a human-level macro regime gate that a weekly SMA would approximate algorithmically.
Full automation of the cycle gate is EXP-023 (future work).

**Why deploy anyway at EXP-020?**  
We are deploying to PAPER trading. Paper trading cannot lose real capital. The purpose of
the paper trading period is to measure execution quality, slippage, and psychological experience
— not to prove cycle durability. The cycle durability problem is documented, monitored manually,
and will be addressed before any live capital allocation.
