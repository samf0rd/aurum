# EXP-005: M15 baseline — EXP-002 config ported to intended timeframe

**Date:** 2026-06-07
**Status:** TERMINATED — drawdown circuit breaker fired in week 2, never lifted
**Results file:** results/exp-005.json
**Experiment config:** experiments/exp-005.yaml

## Hypothesis

EXP-002's best H1 config (confirmation=1, donchian-20, ADX≥25, PF 1.17) would
transfer to M15 and allow us to observe the M15 structural identity (which H1
research had been blocking by running on the wrong timeframe entirely).

| Window | 2020-01-01 → 2025-12-31 |

| research_mode | false (production risk limits) |

## What happened

The backtest ran with production risk engine settings calibrated for H1 cadence:
- Daily loss limit: 2% of equity
- Weekly loss limit: 5% of equity
- Drawdown circuit breaker: 15% from peak → resume when within 10%
- Consecutive loss hard limit: 7 trades

On M15, Donchian-20 generates ~4× more signals per day than on H1 (5-hour lookback
vs 20-hour lookback). This means H1-calibrated daily/weekly limits fire on the first
day of trading. The engine hit DAILY_LOSS_LIMIT after trade 4 on Jan 7, 2020. The
DRAWDOWN_CB fired at 15.59% drawdown approximately 2-3 weeks into the run.

Once the drawdown CB fires, it requires **both**:
1. ≥ 20 trading sessions elapsed (satisfied immediately)
2. Drawdown ≤ 10% from peak (NEVER satisfied — no trades can fire to recover equity)

Result: the engine entered a permanent halt state. The CB counter ran to ~1400+
trading days (5.5 years) without ever lifting. The results JSON contains only the
early trades from January 2020 before the CB fired.

## Diagnostic finding: parameter mismatch, not strategy failure

This result is NOT evidence the M15 strategy is bad. It is evidence that:

1. **H1 risk parameters ≠ M15 risk parameters.** The 2% daily loss limit makes
   sense when you might trade 5-8 times per day on H1. On M15 with 4× more
   signals, 2% is hit after 1-2 losses and 1 big winner = zero activity for the
   rest of the day.

2. **The drawdown CB is non-recoverable without trading.** Once fired, the CB
   requires equity recovery to lift — but equity only changes through trading.
   With no trades allowed, the system can never recover. This is a design property
   that is correct for live trading (don't compound losses) but makes multi-year
   backtests uninformative.

3. **M15 requires its own risk calibration.** Bar cadence is 4× faster. To get
   equivalent "daily" limits you'd need 4× higher percentage thresholds — or
   rethink the limit in terms of number of trades rather than % of equity.

## What this rules out

- You cannot evaluate M15 strategy quality using H1 risk parameters.
- EXP-005's PF and trade counts are NOT representative of the M15 structural edge.
- The "H1 findings don't transfer to M15" hypothesis cannot be tested this way.
- The top-3 winners are all COVID-crisis trades — this window is not representative.

## What EXP-005 DOES tell us (cautiously)

With only 99 trades over 2 months of COVID volatility:
1. The short side (TRENDING_BEAR) shows PF 0.45 — *worse* than H1's 0.53. If
   this holds on longer data, M15 shorts are even weaker than H1 shorts.
2. Both sides lose. EXP-001 (M15 long-only, 1-year, no confirmation) showed
   shorts PF 1.21, longs PF 0.31. EXP-005 is the opposite pattern — but in
   a 2-month COVID window, not valid evidence either way.
3. The false-breakout rate (80%) and 25+ bar winner pattern are consistent
   with the original M15 diagnostic from EXP-001.
