Donchian-20 with ADX≥25, 1-day confirmation bar) on daily bars, 2015-01-01 → 2019-12-31.
1,300 daily bars loaded; ~200 bars warmup (SMA-200) leaves ~1,100 active bars.

## Why this result is INCONCLUSIVE

**11 trades over 5 years cannot support statistical inference.**

With 11 trades and a 45.5% win rate, the binomial confidence interval on win rate is
roughly 17%–77% at 95% confidence. The PF of 2.22 is entirely explained by one trade
(the 2019 breakout at R=+6.20). Remove that single trade and PF ≈ 0.99.

For comparison, the 2020-2025 M15 backtest (EXP-010) had **936 trades** — 85× more
signal events. That is what separates statistical noise from a valid distribution.

The 11-trade result cannot tell us:
- Whether 2015-2019 would have been positive or negative on M15
- Whether the observed PF 2.22 is repeatable or just luck (11 trades, it's luck)
- Whether the system would have survived 2015-2018 gold bear conditions

---

## What we CAN conclude

**1. The strategy does not blow up on daily bars in the gold bear market.**
Max drawdown across the entire 2015-2019 period is -4.3%. On daily bars with ~1%
risk per trade, position sizing keeps drawdowns small regardless of market regime.
This is not a structural result about the strategy — it reflects position sizing.

**2. 2016-2017 are mildly negative, 2018-2019 positive.**
The direction is consistent with gold's price action but with such few trades,
this pattern is not confirmatory.

**3. 2019 breakout fires correctly.**
The June 2019 gold breakout ($1,280 → $1,550) was a genuine gold regime change
(Fed pivot, trade war). The Donchian-20 breakout caught it (R=+6.20). This is
the same type of macro event the strategy captures in 2020-2025.

**4. The daily bars result does NOT proxy for M15.**
On M15, 2015-2019 would have generated ~5× more signals per year — perhaps 600-800
trades total. The distribution would look completely different from 11 trades.

## Conclusions and what EXP-014 actually proves

EXP-014 was designed to test: *"Is this strategy a gold-bull-regime-only system?"*

We cannot answer that question from daily data. The test is valid in structure but
invalid in sample size. The result is a data access limitation, not a strategy finding.

**What to do about EXP-014:**
To run a proper pre-2020 test, we need M15 historical data from 2015-2019. Options:
1. **Dukascopy free tick data**: Dukascopy provides free historical tick data going back
   to ~2003 for major FX/gold pairs. A Dukascopy downloader could reprocess tick data
   into M15 bars and cache them in the same format as the existing Twelve Data cache.
   This would fully enable EXP-014 on the appropriate timeframe.
2. **Upgrade Twelve Data plan**: Some paid plans include longer M15 history.

Until M15 pre-2020 data is available, EXP-014 remains **INCONCLUSIVE**.

### The EXP-013 + EXP-014 synthesis

EXP-013 (annual breakdown) already partially answers the EXP-014 question:
- 2024 was gold's best bull year (+27%) but the strategy LOST (-$17,318, PF 0.76)
- This means the strategy is NOT simply "gold bull market sensitive"
- The strategy is "explosive macro gold move sensitive"

In the 2015-2018 bear market, gold made no explosive upside moves. Long-only breakouts
in that environment would have been a series of failed attempts. EXP-013's 2024
result (gradual bull year = negative) is the best available proxy for 2015-2018.

The most honest estimate: **the strategy would have been net negative for 2015-2018**
(similar to EXP-013's 2021, 2022, and 2024 losses), with potentially one large winner
in 2016 (Brexit gold spike) and one in 2019 (Fed pivot breakout).
